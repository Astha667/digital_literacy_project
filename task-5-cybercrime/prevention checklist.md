UPI Fraud Prevention Checklist (For Students)


General Safety Tips
•	Do not share sensitive details like UPI PIN, OTP, bank account number, or passwords with anyone. 
•	Always verify unknown calls, messages, or emails before taking any action. 
•	Avoid clicking on suspicious links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email. 
•	Install apps only from trusted sources like the Google Play Store or App Store. 
•	Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all financial and important accounts. 
•	Regularly update your phone and apps to the latest version for security patches. 
________________________________________
 UPI & Payment Safety Tips
•	Never approve a “Collect Request” unless you are sure you are paying someone you trust. 
•	Remember: Receiving money does NOT require entering a UPI PIN — if asked, it’s a scam. 
•	Double-check the UPI ID or QR code before making any payment. 
•	Avoid scanning QR codes sent by unknown people for receiving money. 
•	Do not use screen-sharing apps (like AnyDesk, TeamViewer) when instructed by strangers. 
________________________________________Awareness & Good Practices
•	Be cautious of messages creating urgency like “Account will be blocked” or “Offer expires now”. 
•	Keep your social media accounts private to avoid misuse of personal information. 
•	Log out from banking apps when using shared or public devices. 
•	Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts. 
________________________________________
In Case of Fraud
•	Immediately report the incident on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in 
•	Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24x7) 
•	Inform your bank and block your account or UPI services तुरंत. 
•	Keep screenshots and transaction details as evidence.
